# MMR-Prog-Colon: An Interpretable Multimodal Transformer Framework for Joint Prediction of MMR Status and Prognostic Outcomes in Radically Resected Stage II-III Colon Cancer

## Abstract

The TNM staging system inadequately captures tumor heterogeneity, leading to imprecise prognostication and suboptimal adjuvant chemotherapy decisions in stage II–III colon cancer (CC). We developed MMR-Prog-Colon, a Transformer-based multimodal multitask framework for joint prediction of disease-free survival (DFS), overall survival (OS), and mismatch repair (MMR) status. In this multicenter retrospective study, 907 radically resected stage II–III CC patients from four centers (median follow-up: 75.1 months) were divided into training (n=419), internal validation (n=180), and two external test sets (n=236 and n=72). Clinical variables, preoperative CT radiomic features (habitat/peritumoral), and postoperative hematoxylin and eosin (H&E) whole-slide image pathomics were integrated. A Transformer-based early-fusion multitask model was benchmarked against single-modal and late-fusion Stacking models using concordance index (C-index), Kaplan–Meier analysis, and time-dependent receiver operating characteristic (ROC) analysis. Biological interpretability was validated via gradient-weighted class activation mapping (Grad-CAM), Hover-Net, and quantitative immunohistochemistry (qIHC). MMR-Prog-Colon achieved robust DFS prediction (C-index: 0.812–0.884),  outperforming single-modal (ΔC-index: 0.088) and late-fusion Stacking (ΔC-index: 0.024–0.053) models, with minimal cross-center variation (ΔC-index = 0.072). Additionally, it achieved strong predictive performance for OS (C-index: 0.815–0.918) and reliable MMR classification (area under the curve [AUC]: 0.849–0.997). In AI-predicted high-risk patients exhibited immunosuppressive microenvironments characterized by reduced CD3⁺/CD8⁺ T-cell infiltration and elevated FOXP3⁺ regulatory T-cell levels. MMR-Prog-Colon represents an interpretable multimodal multitask Transformer framework for comprehensive prognostication in radically resected stage II–III CC, offering superior predictive performance, biological plausibility, and potential to refine adjuvant chemotherapy stratification and MMR screening.
